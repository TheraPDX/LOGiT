Tests for Pre-Diabetes


Tests that should be given at least once a year
1. Fasting blood sugar
2. AIC
3. Total cholesterol
4. HDL cholesterol
5. Low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol
6. Triglycerides

Each Test Described 
1. A1C (Glycated hemoglobin test) 
- This blood test indicates your average blood sugar level for the past two to three months

How it works:
- It works by measuring the percentage of blood sugar attached to hemoglobin, the oxygen-carrying protein in red blood cells. The higher your blood sugar levels, the more hemoglobin you'll have with sugar attached.

How to Interpret the results:

Normal: < 5.7 %
PreDiabetes: 5.7 - 6.4 %
Type 2 Diabetes: > 6.5 % 

2. Fasting blood sugar test

How to Interpret the results:

Normal: < 100 mg/dL
PreDiabetes: 100 -125 mg/dL
Type 2 Diabetes: > 126 mg/dL

3. Oral glucose tolerance
-rarely used to diagnose diabetes except during pregnancy

How to Interpret the results:

Normal: < 140 mg/dL
PreDiabetes: 140 -199 mg/dL
Type 2 Diabetes: > 200 mg/dL
