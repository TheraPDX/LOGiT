Often Pre-Diabetes has no visible symptoms 
1. One possible sign that you may be at risk of type 2 diabetes is darkened skin on certain parts of the body. This condition is called acanthosis nigricans.  Common areas that may be affected include the neck, armpits, elbows, knees and knuckles.


Ask yuour doctor for a glucose screening of you have any of the risk factors below:

Risk Factors for Pre-Diabetes
1. You're overweight (body mass index above 25)
2. You're inactive
3. You're over 45 yrs old
4. You have a family history of type 2 diabetes
5. You're African-American, Hispanic, American Indian, Asian-American or a Pacific Islander
6. You developed gestational diabetes when you were pregnant or gave birth to a baby who weighed more than 9 pounds (4.1 kilograms)
7. You have polycystic ovary syndrome â€” a condition characterized by irregular menstrual periods, excess hair growth and obesity
8. You have high blood pressure
9. Your high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol is below 35 milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL) (0.9 millimoles per liter, or mmol/L) or your triglyceride level is above 250 mg/dL (2.83 mmol/L)
